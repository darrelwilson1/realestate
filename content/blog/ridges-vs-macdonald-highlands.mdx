I get this question once a month, usually from a relocating tech executive or a family office representative on their second tour. **The Ridges or MacDonald Highlands?** Both are spectacular. Both are guard-gated. Both have wait-lists. They are not the same community.

Let me break it down honestly.

## The view

The Ridges sits on the western edge of Summerlin, terraced into the Spring Mountains foothills. Most lots look east — over Red Rock, the valley, and on a clear morning, the Strip in the distance.

MacDonald Highlands is in Henderson, perched above the valley with **straight-shot Strip views** from nearly every lot. The elevation is higher. The view, frankly, is better if Strip skyline is your priority.

Edge: MacDonald Highlands, for Strip views. Edge: The Ridges, if you want mountain immersion.

## The community

The Ridges is the older of the two — established mid-2000s. It feels lived-in, in a good way. Mature landscaping, established TPC Las Vegas golf course, a strong neighborhood association.

MacDonald Highlands is newer construction overall. More modern architecture. The DragonRidge golf course is excellent, but the country club culture is a notch more formal.

If you want **dinner-party-with-the-neighbors energy**, The Ridges. If you want **architectural showcase**, MacDonald Highlands.

## Price points

This is where it gets interesting. Both communities have homes from ~$2M up to $40M+. The median is roughly comparable — somewhere in the $4-6M range for a custom home.

That said:

- The Ridges has **more inventory**, more turnover, and easier comparables.
- MacDonald Highlands has **more new construction**, more 2020+-vintage homes, and the recent record-setters of the valley.

## My honest take

Both are right answers depending on what you're optimizing for. I take roughly the same number of clients to each. The deciding factor is usually:

1. Strip view vs. mountain view (MacDonald wins; Ridges loses)
2. Established vs. new (Ridges established; MacDonald newer)
3. Country club access (DragonRidge slightly edges TPC for prestige, in my opinion)
